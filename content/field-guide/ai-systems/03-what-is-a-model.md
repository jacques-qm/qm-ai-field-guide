  - q: Does the model think?
    a: >-
      Not the way people do. It predicts text that looks like thinking, and
      often that is useful. But it has no goals, no awareness, and no stake in
      the answer. Treat it as a very capable text engine.
  - q: Does it know today’s news?
    a: >-
      Only up to its training cutoff, unless the harness gives it a tool to look
      things up. On its own, its knowledge is frozen at the moment it was
      trained.
  - q: Can it make things up?
    a: >-
      Yes. It predicts plausible text, and plausible is not always true. That is

A model is the engine at the heart of every AI system. It does one thing, and everything else is built on top of that one thing.

## A next-word predictor

A model is trained on a huge amount of text and code until it gets good at predicting the next piece of text. That is the whole skill. Answering, summarizing, translating, writing code — all of it comes out of that one ability, used over and over. That sounds too simple to be useful. It is enough to do a remarkable amount of work.

## What it knows

As it trains, the model takes in patterns from its data: how language works, a wide store of facts, styles of writing, how code behaves, how an argument holds together. It keeps a compressed version of all of it. When you ask it something, it draws on those patterns rather than looking an answer up in a database. Nothing is filed away to be retrieved. It is closer to something half-remembered and reconstructed on the spot.

## What it can't do alone

A bare model has no memory from one conversation to the next, no access to your files, and no way to do anything in the world. It reads text and writes text. That is all it can reach. The memory, the tools, the ability to act — those come from the harness wrapped around it.

## Why predicting the next word is enough

To predict the next word well, you need a real grip on language, facts, and how reasoning runs. Get good enough at that and you can draft, plan, and explain. There is no separate spark of intelligence hiding underneath. It is a great deal of the right training aimed at one deceptively plain task, pushed far enough that the output starts to look like thought.
