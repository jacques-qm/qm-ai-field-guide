The most important thing about an AI agent is not the machine. It is the gate where you stay in control. Here is the loop.

## Receive a goal

Work arrives the way it already arrives in your business: an email lands, a message comes in, or a schedule says it is time. No new habits required.

## Think, plan, use tools

The agent reads the request, pulls in context, and decides what needs to happen. Then it works with the systems you have connected it to, and **only** those systems, with only the permissions you have granted.

## Draft, and stop

The agent prepares the finished product, whether a reply, a document, or a calendar entry, and then **stops**. Nothing leaves the building yet. The draft goes into a queue and you get a ping.

## The human gate

You look at what it prepared. This takes seconds, not the hour the work would have taken. Approve, edit then approve, or reject. Your name stays on the work. As trust builds, routine low-risk actions can be pre-approved, but anything that leaves your organization passes through you first. That is policy, not a setting someone forgot.

## Execute, and remember

Only after approval does the agent send, file, or update. Every action is logged. Then it writes down what happened, what you approved and what you changed, and your edits become its standing instructions. It does not learn by magic. It keeps **memory**, the way a good employee keeps notes.
