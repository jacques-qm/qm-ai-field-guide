A recommendation is correct and inert. An outcome is something that works on Monday. The distance between them is the whole game.

## The deck that changes nothing

You can buy a perfect analysis of your problem and watch nothing happen. The recommendation was never the hard part. Building the thing, in your real systems, and getting it across the line was.

## Where the work actually is

The difficulty hides in the specifics: your messy data, your odd tools, the exception nobody documented. Advice skips all of it. Outcomes have to survive it.

## The handoff tax

Every handoff loses something. Strategy to spec, spec to build, build to production. The person who understood the problem ends up three steps from the person shipping the fix. Outcomes shrink that distance to zero.

## Buy the result, not the report

Pay for the thing working, not the plan to make it work. It is a higher bar. It is also the only one that moves the number.
